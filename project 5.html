About Company

Apple Inc. was founded in 1976 by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne in Cupertino, California. The company started with the Apple I and Apple II computers, which helped launch the personal computing era. In 1984, Apple introduced the Macintosh, a revolutionary computer with a graphical user interface. After struggles in the 1990s, Steve Jobs returned in 1997 and revitalized the company. Apple launched the iMac in 1998, marking a new era of colorful, consumer‑friendly design. The iPod in 2001 transformed digital music and led to the rise of iTunes. In 2007, Apple released the iPhone, reshaping the smartphone industry forever. The iPad followed in 2010, creating the modern tablet market. Apple also developed wearable technology like the Apple Watch and AirPods. Its ecosystem integrates hardware, software, and services such as iCloud, Apple Music, and Apple Pay. Apple Park, the company’s futuristic headquarters, opened in 2017 in Cupertino. Tim Cook has been CEO since 2011, guiding Apple to become a $2 trillion‑plus company. Today, Apple is recognized worldwide for innovation, design excellence, and loyal customer culture.
